Festivals & Traditions

Bayramlar

Hıdırlez and Navrez, weddings and namings, and the May 18 commemoration of Sürgün.

The Crimean Tatar year carries Islamic feasts, Turkic seasonal celebrations, and one solemn day of remembrance.

Late spring — 5–6 May

Hıdırlez

The major spring festival, celebrating the awakening of nature and the meeting of the saints Hızır and İlyas. People dress in their best clothes, gather in the meadows, light bonfires, and roll Easter-style decorated eggs down hills. Children jump fires and make wishes.

Spring equinox — 21 March

Navrez

The Crimean Tatar New Year, shared with many Iranic and Turkic peoples. Houses are cleaned, new clothes are made, the table holds seven "S" foods (samanu, sumak, sirke, sib, sir, sumbül, sikke) — a feast that welcomes the year to come.

Autumn — September/October

Derviza

The harvest festival. Communities mark the end of the agricultural year with thanksgiving, sheep-shearing games, and traditional wrestling. The festival was suppressed under Soviet rule and revived after 1991.

18 May

Sürgün Day

Sürgünlik Künü — the day of mourning. Crimean Tatars worldwide gather to commemorate the 1944 deportation. Black ribbons, candle vigils, public readings of survivor testimonies, and the unanswerable question: what could have been?

Variable (Islamic calendar)

Ramazan Bayramı

The festival ending the holy month of Ramazan. Houses are cleaned, sweets are prepared, children visit elders and receive small gifts. Special prayers at the Han Cami in Bahçesaray and other historic mosques.

Variable (Islamic calendar)

Qurban Bayramı

The festival of sacrifice, when families share lamb with neighbours, the poor, and extended family. Ritual prayer at sunrise, three days of visits and feasting.
