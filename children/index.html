Children's Corner

Balalar köşesi

Çora Batır, Altın Beşik, ayneni — the stories and songs Crimean Tatar grandparents pass on.

Folk tales, lullabies, nursery rhymes, and the picture dictionary — the building blocks of a child's relationship with Crimean Tatar language and culture.

Folk tales

Çora Batır

The hero of Crimean Tatar oral tradition. A foundling raised by an old couple grows into the warrior who liberates the Crimean Tatar people from their enemies. The destan (heroic poem) cycles around Çora's battles, his magical horse, and his eventual fate.

Tepegöz

The one-eyed giant — a story shared across Turkic peoples. Tepegöz holds shepherds captive in his cave; only the cleverest can blind him and escape past his flocks. A Crimean Tatar parallel to the Cyclops episode in Homer.

Altın Beşik

"The golden cradle." A magical infant in a golden cradle is hidden in the mountains of Crimea, watched over by spirits. Whoever finds the cradle finds the future of the people — but no one is yet worthy. A story of patience, exile, and return.

Üç Qardaş

"Three brothers." The youngest and gentlest brother proves wiser and more courageous than the older two. A classic Crimean Tatar tale of fairness, cleverness, and respect for one's elders.

Lullabies (Ayneni)

The Crimean Tatar lullaby form is called ayneni — short, sweet, repetitive verses that mothers and grandmothers have sung for centuries. The classic refrain is "Ayneni, ayneni, balam ayneni" ("Hush, hush, my child").

Ayneni, ayneni,
Balam ayneni —
Anañ keldi, qaytarmadı
Ayrılmaz balasını…
