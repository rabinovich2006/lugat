Influential People

Mehmet adamlarımız

12 figures across six centuries — and 16 fuller biographies from the source dictionary.

Khans, reformers, writers, dissidents, pilots, singers — a people seen through the lives that shaped it.

1397–1466

Hacı I Geray

Founder of the Crimean Khanate

Established an independent Crimean Tatar state in 1441 with its capital at Bahçesaray. Founder of the Giray dynasty, which ruled Crimea for more than three centuries.

1445–1515

Mengli I Geray

Khan of Crimea, ally of Moscow

Built the original Khan's Palace, conducted active diplomacy with the Ottoman Empire, Lithuania, Poland, and Moscow, and brought the Khanate to peak political influence.

1501–1551

Sahib I Geray

Reformer, builder of the Khan's Palace

Famed Khan who consolidated the Hansaray in Bahçesaray, codified law, and modernised the Khanate's military organisation.

1851–1914

İsmail Gaspıralı

Educator, publisher, pan-Turkic reformer

Founder of the newspaper Tercüman (1883) and the Jadid school movement. Father of modern Crimean Tatar national identity.

1885–1918

Noman Çelebicihan

First Mufti of Crimea, anthem author

President of the short-lived Crimean People's Republic, author of "Ant etkenmen" — the Crimean Tatar national anthem. Executed by Bolsheviks in 1918.

b. 1943

Mustafa Cemilev

Soviet dissident, human-rights leader

Spent fifteen years in Soviet prisons and camps for organising the Crimean Tatar return movement. Long-time chair of the Mejlis, member of Ukrainian Parliament, recipient of the Polish Solidarity Prize.

b. 1957

Refat Çubarov

Chair of the Mejlis since 2013

Politician and member of the Ukrainian Parliament, current chair of the Mejlis of the Crimean Tatar People, prominent voice for Crimean Tatar rights after the 2014 annexation.

b. 1983

Jamala (Susana Jamaladinova)

Singer, Eurovision 2016 winner

Won the Eurovision Song Contest 2016 with the song "1944" — about her great-grandmother's deportation — bringing Crimean Tatar history to a global audience. Composer, jazz musician, public figure.

b. 1972

Akhtem Seitablaev

Actor, filmmaker

Director of the feature film "Haytarma" (2013), the first dramatic feature about the 1944 deportation. Crimean Tatar actor of stage and screen.

b. 1966

Lenür Islâmov

Founder of ATR television

Crimean Tatar businessman and media figure. Founded ATR — the first Crimean Tatar television channel — which was forced into exile after the 2014 annexation.

1920–2011

Cengiz Dağcı

Novelist of the deportation

Author of "Korkunç Yıllar" (Terrible Years), "Yurdunu Kaybeden Adam" (The Man Who Lost His Country), and "Onlar da İnsandı" (They Were People Too).

1920–1971

Amet-Han Sultan

Twice Hero of the Soviet Union

Decorated fighter pilot of the Second World War, credited with 30 individual aerial victories. Later a test pilot in Soviet supersonic and missile-system programmes.
