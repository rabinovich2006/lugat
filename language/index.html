The Crimean Tatar Language

Crimean Tatar (Qırımtatar tili) is a Turkic language of the Kipchak branch, spoken by the indigenous people of Crimea and by Crimean Tatar communities across Eurasia. UNESCO lists it as severely endangered.

The language has three main dialects:

  • Northern (Steppe) — closer to Kazakh and Nogay; spoken in the plains of central Crimea.
  • Middle — the basis of the modern literary norm; spoken in the foothills around Bahçesaray, Karasubazar, and Aqmescit.
  • Southern (Coast) — closer to Turkish, reflecting the Ottoman cultural influence on the southern coast of Crimea.

Crimean Tatar is written today in two alphabets — a Latin alphabet (the standard since 1997) and a Cyrillic alphabet still used by older speakers and in legacy publications.
