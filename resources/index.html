Library & Resources

Kütüphane

A curated bibliography, documentary films, podcasts, and active cultural organisations.

Books

  • Cengiz Dağcı — Korkunç Yıllar (Terrible Years, 1956), Yurdunu Kaybeden Adam (1957), Onlar da İnsandı (1958)
  • Brian Glyn Williams — The Crimean Tatars: From Soviet Genocide to Putin's Conquest (Oxford University Press, 2015)
  • Greta Lynn Uehling — Beyond Memory: The Crimean Tatars' Deportation and Return (Palgrave, 2004)
  • Edward Allworth (ed.) — The Tatars of Crimea: Return to the Homeland (Duke University Press, 1998)
  • Veli Bakalov — Balaçıqlar İçün Resimli Luğat (2019) — the source for this site's illustrated dictionary

Films

  • Haytarma (2013) — dir. Akhtem Seitablaev. The first dramatic feature film about the 1944 deportation.
  • Mustafa (2018) — documentary on Mustafa Cemilev's life and the Crimean Tatar national movement.
  • Crimea: A Tale of Survival — multiple documentary works on the contemporary Crimean Tatar experience.

Media and organisations

  • QHA News — Crimean News Agency, in Crimean Tatar, Ukrainian, English, and Russian
  • ATR TV — Crimean Tatar television channel
  • Mejlis of the Crimean Tatar People — official representative body
  • Crimean Tatar Resource Center
  • Hromadske TV — Ukrainian public broadcaster, extensive Crimean Tatar coverage

Academic resources

  • Krymology — encyclopaedia of Crimean studies
  • Crimean Tatar oral history archives at Columbia University Libraries and the University of Toronto
  • UNESCO entry: "Ornek and the knowledge associated with it" (2021)

Contribute to this site

This is an open cultural project. Recipes, family stories, photographs, songs, corrections, translations — all are welcome. Reach out via the GitHub repository: github.com/rabinovich2006/lugat.
