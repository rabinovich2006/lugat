Sürgün · 18 Mayıs 1944

Voices of Sürgün

Sürgün sesleri

A testimonial archive for the 1944 deportation and the long exile that followed.

18 May 1944. Stalin orders the deportation of the entire Crimean Tatar people — every man, woman, child, elder, and infant. NKVD troops carry out the operation in roughly three days. More than 190,000 people are transported in sealed cattle cars to Uzbekistan, the Urals, and Siberia.

By 1949 nearly half of the deported population — approximately 100,000 people — have died of starvation, disease, and exhaustion in the special settlements where they are held under camp regime.

This section will hold a growing archive of first-person memories: written testimonies from survivors and their descendants, audio recordings made by oral historians, and family photographs. If you or your family have a story to share, please reach out through the Library page.

Some of the stories already gathered in Crimean Tatar memory

  • "They gave us fifteen minutes. My grandmother grabbed only the Quran and a sack of dried bread."
  • "On the train, my mother told me to whisper everything in Crimean Tatar so the soldiers would not understand."
  • "My uncle was at the front fighting for the Soviet army. When he wrote to ask where his family was, no one answered him for three years."
  • "In Andijan they put us off the train. The local Uzbek farmers shared their bread with us. We never forgot."
When strangers are coming, they come to your house. They kill you all and say: "we are not guilty." — Jamala, "1944"

A full testimonial archive — with audio, transcripts, and translations — is in development. Please contribute a story if you can.
